What Causes Climate Change?

The leading cause of global warming is human industrialization and land-use changes, which have significantly altered Earth's climate over the past century. These activities pump heat-trapping gases into the atmosphere at an unprecedented rate.
The most significant of these gases is carbon dioxide (CO2), which is released when fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas are burned for energy. Deforestation also contributes to climate change by reducing the number of trees that can absorb CO2 from the atmosphere.

Effects On People And The Planet

Most people think of climate change as a future problem, but it is already affecting people and the planet today.Extreme weather events are becoming more frequent and intense due to global warming, causing widespread damage to communities, ecosystems, and economies.
People all around the world don't seem to understand that all of our mistakes are adding up. These mistakes not only hurt our planet, but also us the people. Damage from hurricanes, floods, and wildfires costs billions, affecting jobs and infrastructure. Entire industries like farming are effected harshly.

Soulutions

To combat climate change, we must be intentional about thinking about our future. There is tons of individual action we can take to help our planet. We can reduce our carbon footprint by using renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, and by making energy-efficient choices in our daily lives.
We can also support policies that promote sustainability and protect natural resources. This includes reducing waste, conserving water, and protecting forests and other ecosystems. By working together, we can create a more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
In conclusion, climate change is a pressing issue that requires immediate action. By understanding its causes and effects, we can take steps to mitigate its impact and create a more sustainable future. It is up to all of us to make a difference, and every action counts.
